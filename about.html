About

Fourier transform infrared attenuated total reflectance spectroscopy (FTIR-ATR) was performed by Yaroslav Kosobrodov during his work experience in Applied Plasma and Physics Lab, School of Physics, University of Sydney. The spectra were processed with the help of Dr. Elena Kosobrodova who has created this website to assist honor and PhD students with interpretation of FTIR-ATR spectra of polymers. All images of polymer chemical structures except for PU and PES were taken from Wikipedia.

All polymers ecxept for PCL and PU were obtained from GoodFellow (Cambridge, UK). PCL was ordered from Sigma-Aldrich (Australia). PU was provided by Smith & Nephew (Australia). FTIR-ATR spectra were measured using a Digilab FTS7000 spectrometer fitted with a multiple bounce ATR accessory (Harrick, USA) with a trapezium germanium crystal at an incidence angle of 45Â°. To obtain sufficient signal/noise ratio, 500 scans were taken at a resolution of 4 cm-1.
